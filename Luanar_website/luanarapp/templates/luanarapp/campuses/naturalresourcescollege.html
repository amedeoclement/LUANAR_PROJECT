Welcome to LUANAR | Natural Resources College

The Natural Resources College (NRC) was established in 1969 by the Ministry of Agriculture in Malawi to provide certificate level training for technical assistants in the agricultural sector. The college was built on a land previously occupied by Colby college, which had been established in 1956. In 1979, the Government of Malawi, with the support from the Canadian international Development Agency (CIDA), developed the college further, and it began enrolling students in 1983.

In 1999, the Malawi Government restricted the NRC into a semi-autonomous institution, and by 2001 it was registered as Public Trust under the Trustees Incorporation act 1962. This change allowed the NRC to expand its training programs to include agricultural, environmental, and natural resources management disciplines. On December 14, 2014, NRC was integrated into the Lilongwe University of Agriculture and Natural Resources (LUANAR), becoming its second campus.
