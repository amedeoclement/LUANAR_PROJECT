University Clinics

The University clinic caters to the health needs of its students, staff, and sometimes the surrounding community. We aim to provide your confidential service to support your physical and mental well-being Here are some common services that the university clinics offer:

Primary Healthcare

The clinic provides primary healthcare services, including treatment for common illnesses, injuries, and routine health check-ups

Emergency Services

In case of medical emergencies on campus, the clinic offers emergency medical care and first aid before referring the patient to a larger medical facility if needed.

Reproductive Health Services

The clinic offers services related to reproductive health, including contraception, pregnancy testing, and counseling.

Health Education

The clinic conducts health education programs and workshops to promote healthy living and disease prevention among students and staff.

Counselling Services

Mental health support services are provided to help students cope with stress, anxiety, depression, or other mental health issues.

Immunization and Vaccinations

The clinic offers immunizations and vaccinations to ensure that students and staff are up to date on required vaccinations.

Chronic Disease Management

The clinic also assists with the management of chronic health conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, epilepsy, and asthma.

Laboratory Services

Basic laboratory tests are conducted at the clinic for the diagnosis of common illnesses and conditions.

URGENT AND WALK-IN CARE

Walk-in medical appointments are available for staff and students during regular hours. Walk-in visits are for urgent concerns and the clinician may only see you for one concern during the visit. If you have already been seen by a clinician in the clinic for your concern, please book an appointment for follow-up.

Whether you are feeling unwell or it's time for a regular check-up, the physicians and nurses at the Health Centres are here to help and to answer any questions you have relating to your health. As a member of the LUANAR community, you are welcome to use the Health Centre as your primary health care facility for the duration of your time at the university.

We strive to provide comprehensive, patient-centered care by providing a wide range of professional, friendly, compassionate, and confidential services.

YOUR CARE TEAM

The Health center's healthcare team, which is made up of physicians, nurses, and administrative and support staff, is dedicated to providing the College communities with comprehensive, coordinated, and continuing care. The clinic refers patients to specialists or hospitals for further evaluation and treatment if needed, and provides follow-up care when necessary.

All students need to register with the University Medical Scheme (UNIMED) while studying at LUANAR. Each student is encouraged to apply for a UNIMED card and forms are available at the campus clinic. The University clinic has also started accepting those who are on the MASM medical scheme.

House Keeping

Housekeeping plays an important role in maintaining the cleanliness, hygiene, and overall organization of the university campus. Their responsibilities go beyond basic cleaning tasks and encompass a range of duties that contribute to the smooth functioning of the university environment.

Waste Management

Housekeepers manage waste disposal activities on campus, including collecting and disposing of garbage, recycling materials, and ensuring compliance with waste management regulations. They also maintain cleanliness in outdoor areas and manage litter collection.

Room Setup and Maintenance

Housekeeping staff assist in setting up rooms for events, meetings, conferences, and other activities held on campus. They arrange furniture, equipment, and necessary supplies according to specific requirements and ensure that spaces are well-maintained during the events.

Compliance and Safety

Housekeepers ensure that cleaning activities are carried out in compliance with health and safety. They use appropriate cleaning products and equipment, follow safety protocols, and take necessary precautions to prevent accidents or hazards in the workplace.

Cleaning and Sanitization

Housekeeping staff are responsible for cleaning classrooms, offices, common areas, restrooms, laboratories, and other facilities across the campus. They ensure that these spaces are regularly cleaned, sanitized, and maintained to provide a safe and healthy environment for students and staff.
